The Problem

hiding in plain sight.

When a referral arrives at your practice, someone on your team decides how urgently to schedule it. That decision is based on instinct, experience, and whatever made it into the referral note — which is rarely complete.

How It Works

From referral to
scheduled priority in seconds.

01
Referral Arrives
A new patient referral enters your practice. Velox reads the associated EHR record automatically — labs, vitals, diagnoses, medications.
02
Guideline Scoring
Velox scores the patient against the current clinical guidelines for their pathway — GOLD 2026, AGA 2026, ESC/ERS 2022, GINA 2025, and more.
03
Decision Trace
A step-by-step clinical reasoning trace shows your MA exactly how the score was reached — every lab value, every threshold, every decision point.
04
Priority Set
The patient is assigned a scheduling priority — Routine, Standard, Priority, or Critical — using your clinic's own thresholds, not just the guideline defaults.
Capabilities

Built for the way
specialist practices actually work.

Deterministic Scoring
No black boxes. Every scoring decision is rule-based and traceable. Your MA can read the reasoning. Your physician can trust it. Your attorney can defend it.
On-Premise Security
Velox runs on a local Linux device inside your practice. No PHI leaves the building. No cloud dependency. No internet required for operation.
Clinic Preferences
Your thresholds, your rules. Override any guideline default with practice-specific criteria — combinatorial rules, custom priority tiers, order preferences.
Suggested Orders
Velox identifies missing data and surfaces the specific orders needed to complete the workup — before the patient's first visit, not after.
EHR Integration
Reads directly from your existing EHR via FHIR R4 API. Compatible with Epic and major EHR platforms. No manual data entry. No duplicate systems.
Pathway Validation
Every scoring pathway is validated against canonical clinical cases before deployment. You can run your own truth tables and add test cases through the UI.
